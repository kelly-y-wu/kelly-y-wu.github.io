PhD Candidate

Welcome! I am a PhD Candidate in the Agricultural and Resource Economics Department at UC Davis. My research interests include land use, forestry, coastal climate resilience, and remote sensing. My dissertation focuses on evaluating the impact of resource conservation and management policies in Indonesia and the U.S., providing insights into adaptation strategies for global climate change.

I am on the job market 2024-2025. Please feel free to contact me via kelwu@ucdavis.edu.

Research
Job Market Paper

Tropical forest conservation and fires: Evidence from Indonesia

This study evaluates the impact of Indonesia's forest moratorium on fire activity, where fire is commonly used for agricultural preparation. While the moratorium has halted land conversions in protected areas, it has not achieved expected environmental benefits, such as reduced fires and smoke. The puzzle is unpacked utilizing the agricultural land use change captured by a time series of high-resolution Earth observations.

Work in Progress

Spatial, temporal, and cross-fishery spillovers in the U.S. West Coast Dungeness Crab Fishery

The welfare of fishing communities along the U.S. West Coast is increasingly at risk due to climate shocks. The North Pacific marine heatwave has disrupted the Dungeness crab fishery, which accounts for over 25% of the region’s fishing revenue. How has this climate event reshaped the distribution of fishery income?
